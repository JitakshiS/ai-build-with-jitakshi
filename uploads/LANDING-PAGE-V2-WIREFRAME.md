# Landing Page v2 – Creative Wireframe & Design Brief

## Design Direction

**Reference sites (Orizon.co featured):**
- **karlgonsalves.com** – typographic personality, playful micro-interactions, handcrafted feel, motion reel that loops without friction. THIS is the closest vibe to what we want.
- **joinclyde.com** – polished but never sterile. Smart illustrations, modular scrollable blocks. Good for the session tier section.
- **animejs.com** – every animation demonstrates the product itself. The page IS the proof. For us: the page itself should demonstrate the quality difference between generic AI training and what Jitakshi delivers.
- **thefoundation.house** – tiered product cards with real-time metrics, glassmorphism. Good reference for the session format comparison.
- **silencio.es** – bold typography, motion-heavy, playground-like. Sound effects with scrolling.
- **unveil.fr** – portfolio as cinematic story with visual chapters. Full-screen visuals, narrative scroll progression.

**The core idea:** The site itself is the proof of quality. If someone lands on this page and thinks "wow, this is different from every other AI training page I've seen" – that's the same reaction they'll have in the session. The medium IS the message.

---

## Visual Style

- **Background:** Warm cream/off-white (#faf9f7). NOT pure white. Feels like paper.
- **Typography:** Caveat (handwritten) for annotations, highlights, and accent text. Inter for body. Handwritten elements should feel like someone marked up a document with a pen – underlines, circles, arrows, crossed-out text.
- **Colors:** Black (#0a0a0a), cream, accent green (#c8ff00). Minimal palette. No blue, no gradients.
- **Feel:** Like opening someone's personal notebook. Annotations in the margins. Arrows pointing to things. Handwritten notes next to clean type. Not messy – intentional and designed, but feels human.
- **Animations:** Scroll-triggered reveals. Text that writes itself in. Annotations that draw themselves. Cards that slide in. NOT fade-up-from-below on everything (that's the generic approach).
- **Mobile:** Must work beautifully on mobile. All interactions should have touch equivalents. No hover-dependent features.

## Page Structure (10 sections)

### SECTION 1: THE HOOK (full viewport)
**Goal:** Stop the scroll in 3 seconds.

- Full screen, centered
- Big handwritten text: **"Your team did the AI training. Nothing changed on Monday."**
- Small clean text below: "Because they learned about AI. They didn't learn to build with it."
- No name, no logo, no CTA. Just the problem.
- Subtle animated scroll indicator (hand-drawn arrow bouncing gently)
- **Animation:** The text writes itself in, character by character, like someone writing on a whiteboard

### SECTION 2: THE SHIFT (scroll-triggered)
**Goal:** In 10 seconds, explain why everything they've tried hasn't worked and what's different.

- Left side: "What most teams do" – crossed out in handwritten red/gray
  - Sat through briefings ~~checked~~
  - Tried prompt engineering ~~checked~~
  - Watched demos ~~checked~~
  - Nothing changed ~~checked~~
- Right side: "What actually works" – highlighted in green
  - Your team builds a real tool
  - Using AI, with their own hands
  - Solving their own problems
  - In a private, structured session
- **Animation:** The checkmarks appear as you scroll. The cross-outs animate. The green highlights draw themselves.
- Handwritten annotation arrow connecting the two sides: "this is the gap"

### SECTION 3: WHO I AM (dark card, asymmetric)
**Goal:** Establish trust in 5 seconds. Not a bio – a proof of legitimacy.

- Circular headshot (headshot.jpeg) with handwritten arrow: "that's me"
- Handwritten: "I'm Jitakshi."
- Three short lines (not paragraphs):
  - "I'm Anthropic's Claude Ambassador for Atlantic Canada."
  - "100+ people have shipped real software in my sessions."
  - "I've partnered with Shopify, YWCA, Volta, Lovable, v0, Onside, and Claude Code."
- Credential pills in a row
- LinkedIn link
- Handwritten note in corner: "previously: Google, Meta, Netflix, Audi"
- **NO long paragraphs. Three lines max.**

### SECTION 4: PHOTO GALLERY (full-width, immersive)
**Goal:** Show the energy. Make them want to be in the room.

- 4 photos in a cinematic layout (not a basic grid)
  - event-claude-code-2.jpg (main/large)
  - event-v0-vercel.jpg
  - event-cursor-hack.jpeg
  - event-hackathon-group.jpeg
- Handwritten captions on each: "claude code masterclass", "v0 by vercel", "cursor hack day", "hackathon"
- **Animation:** Photos slide in from different directions as you scroll
- No section heading needed – the photos speak

### SECTION 5: PICK YOUR DEPTH (interactive – the centerpiece)
**Goal:** This is where they decide which format fits. Make it feel like choosing, not buying.

**INTERACTIVE CONCEPT – "The Timeline":**
- A horizontal slider or click-through that shows what happens at each depth
- Three states: 2 hours / Full day / 4 weeks
- When you select one, the content transforms:

**2 Hours – "The Door Opener"**
- Visual: A single app being built in fast-forward (GIF or animation)
- Tools: Lovable logo
- What happens: "Your team builds their first thing. They see what's possible."
- Outcome (handwritten): "They walk out curious."

**Full Day – "The Capability Builder"**
- Visual: Multiple apps being built, more complex (GIF or animation)
- Tools: Lovable + Claude Code logos
- What happens: "Everyone builds and deploys a working tool. Your team stops waiting on dev."
- Outcome (handwritten): "They walk out capable."

**4 Weeks – "The Culture Shift"**
- Visual: An entire workflow transformation (GIF or animation)
- Tools: Lovable + Claude Code + AI Agents logos
- What happens: "Your team builds tools for real business problems. AI becomes how your org works."
- Outcome (handwritten): "They walk out different."

- "most teams start here" annotation on Full Day
- Pricing note: "Pricing depends on your team. Let's figure it out together."
- **This section should feel like an experience, not a pricing table**

### SECTION 6: THE LONG GAME (warm cream card)
**Goal:** Sell the culture shift, not the session.

- Handwritten heading: "This isn't a workshop you forget by Friday."
- 4 outcomes with handwritten icons/annotations:
  1. "Everyday tasks get automated" – your team starts building tools for the boring stuff
  2. "Creative time goes up" – less routine, more thinking
  3. "Problems get solved in hours, not weeks" – no more waiting on the dev queue
  4. "It spreads" – one person shows another. That's how culture changes.
- **Animation:** Each outcome reveals as you scroll, with a hand-drawn connecting line between them

### SECTION 7: WOMEN FOCUSED (with Volta sprint photo)
**Goal:** Differentiation. No one else has this evidence.

- volta-sprint-cohort.jpeg (full-width, cinematic)
- Handwritten heading: "Built for everyone. Proven with women first."
- Short text: "10 women. 4 weeks. Zero coding experience. Every one of them deployed a working app."
- Stats: 100+ shipped / 10 sprint graduates / 6 partners
- Handwritten annotation on the photo pointing to the group: "the first cohort"
- **This section is about the story, not the stats**

### SECTION 8: PROOF (LinkedIn screenshots)
